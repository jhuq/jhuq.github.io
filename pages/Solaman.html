Solaman/Sasha

Pakistsan Wedding Events

Solaman married a few years ago in Pakistan.
Both events are culturally and traditionally influenced. To understand the importance, it is best to understand the Pakistani culture. Marriage is not only considered a union between husband and wife, but also a union between their families.

A Pakistani wedding consists of different events combined in week-long celebrations. However, in Solaman and Sasha case, the Walima and Rukhsati events were held the following year. Let me walk you through traditions as they pertain to Solaman and Sasha's wedding and you can decide for yourself how colorful these events can be.

Mehdi

The Mehndi ceremony is held the night before the Nikah as a way of wishing good health and success as they make their journey on to the marriage. The Mehndi event was for close family members and friends.
This was the first main function of the wedding where the bride gets her hand hennaed with a beautiful mehndi design. It was just a fun night of dance and music. The girls perform dances that they spend weeks preparing. There was a henna artist who applied Mehndi to our hands during the event.

Sasha is wearing a traditional orange salwar kami dress. There are no set style requirements, however, wearing bright colors such as yellow or orange is common for the Mendi event.

Nikah

The Nikah is the formal marriage ceremony where a marriage contract is signed by both the bride and the groom in the presence of close family members. The Nikah is typically performed by a religious scholar at a mosque, who is licensed by the government to perform the ceremony.

The Nikah was a small intimate event that involves just families and close friends to celebrate the marriage of the bride and groom. The dresses of Nikah are usually of pastel colors, Sasha is wearing gray with a white dupatta. Solaman is wearing a white salwa kurta.

Baraat/Niki Reception

The Barat is the reception of the groom, his family, and his relatives that take place after the Nikah. This event was arranged by the family of the bride. Arrangements included the wedding hall, food, and photography.
Here we arrived on a horse-drawn carriage taking us to the Nikah reception event. As we got closer to the hall we were surrounded by the beat of drums. Solaman wore a traditional sherwani which is a knee-length coat buttoning to the neck and pink turban.

Walima

The Walima was the last formal event which is hosted by the bridegroom and his family. This is a way of welcoming Sasha and her family into our family; the celebration includes relatives and friends as the guests of both families. There is food served and entertainment during this occasion.
Our family greeted guests by throwing roses over their heads. Our family also present garland to the bride's Mother and Father which is worn around the necks. When Solaman and Sasha arrived, Sidra my youngest daughter was given the basket of roses to throw at the bride and groom. But rather than throwing over their head she being mischievous, took handfuls and threw right at her brother getting flower peddles in his hair.
